GUIDELINE TO HALT MORATORIUM PERIOD

Dear Decadev,

If you are currently employed but your loan is still within it’s moratorium period we advise that you halt your moratorium and begin repayment, this is due to the fact that as you already know your loan accrues interest during the moratorium period.Doing this would help you pay off your loan faster thereby reducing the total amount paid on interest.

If you would like to halt your moratorium kindly follow the below steps:

  1. indly refer HERE for the document title Request to halt Moratorium Sterling Bank, download it and adapt it, to suit your request ensure you look out for the dates and update appropriately also make sure that you sign. Send an email to decagonsupport@sterling.ng cc emilia.Okwong@sterling.ng and fredricae@decagonhq.com requesting to halt your moratorium.
  2. Kindly include a copy of your employment offer letter to assist the bank ascertain that you are currently in employment.
  3. Your request will be reviewed and feedback provided to you. If you don’t receive fee any communication within 48 working hours of sending the email please feel free to send a reminder.

GUIDELINE FOR INCREASING MONTHLY LOAN REPAYMENT AMOUNT

Dear Decadev,

This request is to increase the amount you pay on a monthly basis from what it currently is to a higher amount. If you wish to only make a one off increased payment then you should be referring to the section that covers lump sum payments as that is what applies to you.

Please note that you can only increase your monthly repayment amount, you cannot reduce it.

  1. kindly refer HERE for the document title Loan restructuring request Sterling Bank, download it and adapt it, to suit your request ensure you look out for the dates and update appropriately also make sure that you sign. Send an email to decagonsupport@sterling.ng cc emilia.Okwong@sterling.ng and fredricae@decagonhq.com requesting to increase your repayment amount on a monthly basis.
  2. Your email should contain proof that your salary can cover the new amount you are requesting for, this can be in the form of a copy of your offer letter/ a copy of your salary receipt into your sterling bank account.
  3. Your request will be reviewed and feedback provided to you. If you don’t receive feeany communication within 48 working hours of sending the email please feel free to send a reminder.

GUIDELINE FOR BULK SUM PAYMENT

Dear Decadev,

A bulk sum payment is anything from NGN 200,000 which you have at hand and wish to use to reduce your loan amount.

To make a bulk sum payment kindly Fund your individual sterling bank current account (i.e the account you opened at the beginning of the program) with the amount you intend on making a bulk sum payment with. Then refer HERE for the document title Loan Liquidation template sterling bank, download it and adapt it based on your preference and sign. Once completed, the request letter should be sent to the Cafeone team on cafeoneteam@sterling.ng ensure that you cc fredricae@decagonhq.com in the email. Letter should be sent in on the morning of the day you want the request treated.

Kindly note that making a lump sum payment does NOT exempt you from covering your monthly repayment amount. What a lump sum payment does is reduce your Principal amount thereby causing the total amount you pay on interest on your loan and your monthly repayment amount to reduce.
Lump sums reduce your loan principal hence your monthly repayment amount will reduce when you make a lump sum payment. If you wish to increase it back you can send an email to the cafeone team requesting for this. More information on this is provided in the section dedicated to increasing loan repayment amount.

GUIDELINE TO CREATE A STANDING ORDER

Dear Decadev,

Kindly make sure that your salary is domicile in sterling bank according to your loan agreement.

But if you work with a financial institution, an affiliate of a financial institution, or a foreign company and as such cannot get your salary paid into sterling bank, Here is a guide on how to make standing instruction request to your bank, instructing them to transfer an amount sufficient to cover your monthly loan repayment to your sterling bank account.

With a standing order you’re giving your bank permission to make a payment on your behalf to your sterling bank account. Payments should be of the same amount every time, and go out on a regular frequency - once a month on the first of the month, or at the end of the month. make sure there is always sufficient funds available.

Kindly do the following:

  • Head to a branch, or call your bank’s customer service team.
  • Find your bank’s standing order form, which may be online or in hard copy. You can refer HERE for the title Standing order sample form to find a sample of what this form looks like.
  • Complete the standing order request form, including details of the recipient, the amount to be paid and the dates of payments. Please note that the recipient in this case is you i.e your sterling bank current account.
  • Your bank will process and set up the standing order for you - this may take a few days.
  • Kindly follow up to get an acknowledgement that this order has been completed and share a copy of the acknowledgement (this would most likely be a stamped copy fo your form) and share this with faithp@decagonhq.com cc fredricae@decagonhq.com
  • Standing order payments will be sent from your bank account to the designated recipient according to your instructions.
